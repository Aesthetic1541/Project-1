MAJOR SOMNATH SHARMA

1923-1947

First Paramveer Chakra Awardee

Major Somnath Sharma, PVC (1923-1947) was an Indian Army officer who was posthumously awarded the Param Vir Chakra (PVC), India's highest military honor, for his exceptional bravery and sacrifice during the Indo-Pakistani War of 1947.

Early Life and Education

Somnath Sharma was born on January 4, 1923, in Dadh, a small village in Kangra district, Himachal Pradesh, to Major General Amar Nath Sharma and Lakshmi Devi. His family had a strong military tradition, with his father and two brothers serving in the Indian Army.

Sharma completed his schooling at Sherwood College, Nainital, and then joined the Prince of Wales Royal Military College, Dehradun. In 1942, he was commissioned into the 8th Battalion, 19th Hyderabad Regiment (later renamed 4th Battalion, Kumaon Regiment).

Career

During World War II, Sharma served in the Middle East and North Africa. After India gained independence in 1947, he was deployed to Kashmir to combat Pakistani forces.

Battle of Badgam

On November 3, 1947, Sharma's company was tasked with defending the strategic area of Badgam, near Srinagar Airport. Pakistani forces, supported by artillery and mortar fire, launched a fierce attack. Sharma, despite being outnumbered and outgunned, rallied his troops and led a counterattack.

As the enemy closed in, Sharma directed his men to hold their ground, and he himself took up a position with a light machine gun. Despite being wounded, he continued to fight, inspiring his troops to hold their position.

Moments

When Pakistani forces breached the defenses, Sharma ordered his men to fall back while he covered their retreat. Severely wounded and surrounded, he fought valiantly until his ammunition was exhausted.

In his final moments, Sharma radioed his commanding officer, saying: "The enemy is only 50 yards from us. We are heavily outnumbered. We are under devastating fire. I will not withdraw an inch but will fight to the last man."

Vir Chakra Citation

Sharma's citation for the Param Vir Chakra reads:

"Major Somnath Sharma's company was subjected to heavy artillery and mortar fire, which caused heavy casualties. Undeterred by the overwhelming odds, Major Sharma rallied his men and led a counterattack... His courageous leadership inspired his men to fight bravely... Though seriously wounded, he refused to be evacuated... His inspiring example and valiant fighting spirit saved the airport from falling into enemy hands."

Legacy

Major Somnath Sharma's bravery and sacrifice have become legendary in Indian military history. His legacy inspires generations of soldiers and civilians alike. The Indian Army has honored his memory by:

- Renaming the Srinagar Airport road as "Major Somnath Sharma Marg" - Establishing the Major Somnath Sharma, PVC, Memorial in Badgam - Commissioning a statue in his honor at the National War Memorial, New Delhi

Conclusion

Major Somnath Sharma's story embodies the values of courage, sacrifice, and patriotism. His selfless act saved countless lives and secured a strategic location for India. His legacy continues to inspire and motivate future generations to serve their nation with honor and distinction.




मेजर सोमनाथ शर्मा की जीवनी

मेजर सोमनाथ शर्मा एक महान सैनिक थे जिन्होंने अपनी वीरता और बलिदान से भारत की स्वतंत्रता और एकता की रक्षा की। उनका जन्म 31 जनवरी 1923 को जम्मू में हुआ था ¹। उनके पिता अमरनाथ शर्मा एक डॉक्टर थे और सेना में डायरेक्टर जनरल के पद से सेवामुक्त हुए थे।

शिक्षा और सैनिक जीवन

मेजर सोमनाथ शर्मा ने अपनी शुरुआत की स्कूली शिक्षा अलग-अलग जगहों पर प्राप्त की, जहाँ उनके पिता की पोस्टिंग होती थी। उन्होंने शेरवुड कॉलेज नैनीताल और प्रिंस ऑफ वेल्स रेल अकादमी देहरादून से अपनी शिक्षा प्राप्त की थी ¹। उन्होंने 22 फरवरी 1942 को चौथी कुमाऊं रेजिमेंट में बतौर कमीशंड ऑफिसर प्रवेश लिया और मलाया में जापानियों के खिलाफ लड़ाई में भाग लिया ¹।

कश्मीर में बलिदान

3 नवंबर 1947 को मेजर सोमनाथ शर्मा की एक टुकड़ी को कश्मीर घाटी के बदगाम मोर्चे पर जाने का आदेश दिया गया। उन्होंने अपनी टुकड़ी के साथ दुश्मन को बढ़ने से रोके रखा, लेकिन इस दौरान उन्हें खुद गोली लग गई और वे वीरगति को प्राप्त हुए ¹।

परमवीर चक्र

उनके महान बलिदान के लिए भारत सरकार ने उन्हें प्रथम परमवीर चक्र की उपाधि से सम्मानित किया ¹। यह उपाधि भारत में सैनिकों को उनकी वीरता और बलिदान के लिए दी जाती है।

विरासत

मेजर सोमनाथ शर्मा की वीरता और बलिदान ने भारत की स्वतंत्रता और एकता की रक्षा में महत्वपूर्ण भूमिका निभाई। उनकी कहानी आज भी हमें प्रेरित करती है और हमें देश के लिए बलिदान करने की प्रेरणा देती है ।
